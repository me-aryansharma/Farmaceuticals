ABOUT US

We are Team Farmaceuticals consisting of five members, third year engineering students studying in Ajay Kumar Garg Engineering College, Ghaziabad in Uttar Pradesh. We built this project as a part of Smart India Hackathon 2022 contest. We believe that through this approach, we will not only solve the problem of stubble burning and its ill effects on the environment, but we will also help farmers earn some money in return of stubble.

ABOUT OUR PROJECT-

With the help of government we will provide necessary services required by the farmers free of cost with the help of hybrid machine as earlier they used to pay for such services.
These platform will not leave any stubble behind during harvesting, which happens to be scattered in the field when they used to pay for service and after that they burn it.
So on behalf of our service our machine will collect the stubble free of cost and densely pack it for easy transportation in our nearby stubble storage facility.
In our facility we will make very small and dirt free pulp out of the stubble and use it for making biodegradable cutlery which can act as a cheaper and reliable replacement of in service plastic cutlery and the excess pulp will be supplied to ethanol plants.

How Our Hybrid Machine is gonna work?

Machine will harvest the rice crop during kharif season and not leave any straw and stubble behind.
It will also be able to harvest the wheat crop during rabi season.
While harvesting the machine will be simultaneously do digging , stirring in the field.
It will be storing the stubble during harvesting process and densely pack it for transportation.
The machine will also be able to cut corn and sugarcane after some modification in the service center when needed.
The agri waste collected will then be transported to our facility for cutlery production.

Utilizing the stubble in best way possible

In our facility we will make very small and dirt free pulp out of the stubble.
This pulp is then moulded into the shape of cutlery.
Then we can use it for making biodegradable cutlery which can act as a cheaper and reliable replacement of in service plastic cutlery.
Also, the excess pulp can be supplied to ethanol plants for the production of ethanol using this pulp.
